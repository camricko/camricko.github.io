A Labyrinth was an alternate reality game developed by the University of Chicago during the COVID-19 pandemic. It took place between April and May of 2020, inviting approximately 3,500 players to form 73 teams and engage with a digital space for imaginative creation. Teams competed by tackling quests across diverse categories such as making, connecting, moving, performing, solving, worldbuilding, and researching.

This project was my first experience with pixel art, though I was very familiar with the art style from video games. Pixel art presents the challenge of not only representing real-life objects with limited pixels but also thinking with historical conventions of pixel art in other video games to embed associative meaning into visual assets. Striking a balance between resemblance and novelty is an important endeavor in pixel art, and one I welcome.
